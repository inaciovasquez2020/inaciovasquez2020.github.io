The Unified Rigidity Framework (URF) is a structural research program asserting that finite information capacity enforces rigidity across physical, mathematical, and computational systems.

Research Vision

My work formalizes how complexity, entropy, locality, and structural constraints govern natural systems. The objective is to derive universal obstructions — spectral gaps, capacity bounds, and rigidity walls — shared by quantum physics, computation, and geometry.

Spectral Gap Rigidity Wall (Open Problem)

This is the single unresolved core lemma of the Unified Rigidity Framework. All other components reduce to this object.

Canonical entry document: The Spectral Gap Rigidity Wall — Problem Statement (PDF)

Formal Statement

Let \(L = -\Delta + \mathrm{Hess}(\Phi)\) be the rigidity operator acting on the admissible configuration space \(\mathcal{M}\), where \(\Phi\) is the universal rigidity functional. The conjecture is: 

\[ \mathrm{Spec}(L | \ker(\mathrm{Per})^\perp) \subset [\varepsilon_0, \infty) \]

Status

  • Proven: trees, bounded treewidth, random lifts.
  • Empirical: dense cycle expanders.
  • Open: deterministic high-cycle overlap graphs.

How to Attack This Problem

  • Derive combinatorial lower bounds on local-type growth in expanders.
  • Reduce to known conjectures (mass gap, large sieve, expander mixing).
  • Construct explicit counterexamples via covering graphs or lifts.
  • Mechanize finite cases in Lean/Coq.
