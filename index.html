Founder, Unified Rigidity Framework
Independent Researcher in Mathematical Physics & Information Theory

Unified Rigidity Framework (URF) is a structural program asserting that finite information capacity enforces rigidity across physical, mathematical, and computational systems. The focus is on invariants, not algorithms: objects that cannot be bypassed by encoding or computational tricks.

Research Vision

My work formalizes how complexity, entropy, locality, and structural constraints govern natural systems. The objective is to derive universal obstructions — spectral gaps, capacity bounds, and rigidity walls — shared by quantum physics, computation, and geometry.

Core Contributions

  • Quantum Foundations: Operational collapse, irreversibility, finite measurement.
  • Rigidity & Complexity: Unified Rigidity Framework (URF).
  • Entropy & Time: Chronos operator, EntropyDepth bounds.
  • Applied Physics & Biology: Radiative rigidity, biological friction.

URF Verification System (Prototype)

Minimal executable implementation of URF. Generates and verifies cryptographically sealed certificates.

  • Spectral gap certificates (JSON)
  • Schema validation
  • Numerical + structural verification
  • SHA-256 proof sealing

Repository: github.com/inaciovasquez2020/urf-core
