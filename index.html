Research Vision

My work focuses on foundational principles in physics, mathematics, and information theory, aiming to formalize how complexity, capacity, and structural constraints govern natural and computational systems. I develop axiomatic and operational frameworks linking quantum mechanics, entropy, locality, and finite information.

Core Contributions

  • Quantum Foundations. Operational models of wavefunction collapse, irreversibility, and finite-capacity measurement.
  • Rigidity & Complexity. Unified Rigidity Framework (URF): locality–capacity–stability axioms.
  • Entropy & Time. Chronos operator and EntropyDepth bounds.
  • Applied Physics & Biology. Radiative rigidity and biological friction models.

Scientific Impact

  • Refinement of quantum measurement theory.
  • Structural approaches to computational complexity.
  • Precision nuclear and electroweak modeling.
  • Information-driven biophysics.
