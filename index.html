Independent Researcher in Mathematical Physics & Information Theory

Unified Rigidity Framework (URF) is a structural program asserting that finite information capacity enforces rigidity across physical, mathematical, and computational systems. The focus is on invariants, not algorithms: objects that cannot be bypassed by encoding or computational tricks.

Research Vision

My work formalizes how complexity, entropy, locality, and structural constraints govern natural systems. The objective is to derive universal obstructions — spectral gaps, capacity bounds, and rigidity walls — shared by quantum physics, computation, and geometry.

Spectral Gap Rigidity Wall (Open Problem)

This is the single unresolved core lemma of the Unified Rigidity Framework. All other components reduce to this object.

Formal Statement

Let L = -Δ + Hess(Φ) be the rigidity operator on the admissible configuration space M, where Φ is the universal rigidity functional. The conjecture is: 

Spec(L | ker(Per)^⊥) ⊂ [ε₀, ∞)
for some ε₀ > 0 independent of system size.

Status

  • Proven: trees, bounded treewidth, random lifts.
  • Empirical: dense cycle expanders.
  • Open: deterministic high-cycle overlap graphs.

How to Attack This Problem

  • Derive combinatorial lower bounds on local-type growth in cycle-expanders.
  • Reduce to known conjectures (e.g. mass gap, large sieve, expander mixing).
  • Construct explicit counterexamples via covering graphs or lifts.
  • Mechanize small cases in Lean/Coq to detect structural failure modes.

Bounty Framing

  • Formal proof of spectral gap.
  • Explicit counterexample.
  • Reduction to known conjecture.

Solving this closes URF and all derivative rigidity programs.
